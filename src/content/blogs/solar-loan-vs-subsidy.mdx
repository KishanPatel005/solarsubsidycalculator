## Subsidy First, Loan for the Rest

If you are a typical Indian homeowner, the best way to think about rooftop solar payment is:

1) Apply scheme eligibility and compute the **subsidy** (reduces upfront cost).  
2) Decide whether you’ll pay the remaining amount in cash or via **loan/EMI**.  

The central subsidy is what makes the economics attractive for many families. But solar still requires capital, and not everyone wants to spend a lump sum.

## EMI Comparison — Top Banks 2025

Even a small EMI can feel heavy if you don’t compare it with your expected monthly savings. A simple way to judge affordability:

- If monthly EMI is close to monthly savings, you have a “near-neutral cashflow” project.
- If EMI is much higher than savings, you’ll need extra monthly budget until loan ends.
- If you can increase self-consumption (daytime usage), savings increase.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Should I take a loan even if I have savings?

It depends on your comfort and emergency fund needs. A loan helps keep liquidity, but costs interest. Compare total interest vs your opportunity cost.

### Does subsidy reduce my loan amount?

Yes. Subsidy reduces the final cost after verification, so your required loan amount can be lower if you plan correctly.

### What’s the best tenure?

Shorter tenure reduces interest but increases EMI. Longer tenure reduces EMI but increases interest. Use the EMI calculator to test options.

### Can I prepay the loan early?

Some banks allow prepayment with or without charges. Confirm prepayment penalty before signing.
