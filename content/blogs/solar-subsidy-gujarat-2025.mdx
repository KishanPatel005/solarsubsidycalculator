## Solar Subsidy in Gujarat — Overview

Gujarat has been one of the most active states for residential rooftop solar adoption. For homeowners, the **big picture** in 2026 is simple:

- The **central subsidy (PM Surya Ghar / CFA)** reduces your upfront cost.
- Your DISCOM process (feasibility, net metering, commissioning) decides timelines.
- Any additional state-level benefit (if applicable) must be verified from official portals and notifications because policies can change.

## Central + State Subsidy in Gujarat

### Central subsidy (PM Surya Ghar)

The central subsidy is based on system size:

- 1–2 kW gets a higher per-kW benefit (for estimation).
- 3 kW reaches the typical maximum cap.

<InfoBox>
Rule-of-thumb estimation used across guides:  
- Up to 2 kW: ₹30,000 per kW  
- 3rd kW: additional ₹18,000  
- Maximum cap: ₹78,000  
Always verify the final sanctioned amount on the official portal for your approved system size and category.
</InfoBox>

### Gujarat “state benefit”

Gujarat has historically run state rooftop programs (often discussed under “SURYA Gujarat” branding via DISCOM portals). In 2026, the safest approach is:

1) Treat the **central subsidy** as the main confirmed component for estimates.  
2) Treat any additional state incentive as **policy-dependent** and verify before budgeting.

<WarningBox>
Avoid budgeting your project assuming an extra state subsidy amount unless you can see the current slab/rules on official Gujarat DISCOM / state portal pages. Policies and eligibility can vary by consumer category and period.
</WarningBox>

## How to Apply Through GEDA Portal

Many homeowners search “GEDA portal” when they want a Gujarat-specific route. Practically, your steps still revolve around the national portal and DISCOM workflow, but Gujarat has dedicated portals for rooftop coordination.

<StepBox number={1} title="Use the calculator to estimate system size">
Start with your average monthly bill, roof area and sanctioned load. Use <a href="/calculator">our calculator</a> to get an estimated kW and central subsidy. This helps you avoid over-sizing.
</StepBox>

<StepBox number={2} title="Register and apply on the national portal">
Go to <a href="https://pmsuryaghar.gov.in">pmsuryaghar.gov.in</a>, select Gujarat and your DISCOM, and submit the application using your consumer number details.
</StepBox>

<StepBox number={3} title="Follow Gujarat DISCOM rooftop portal instructions">
For Gujarat, you may be guided to DISCOM rooftop portals for vendor selection, feasibility, and net metering steps. Keep screenshots/PDFs of approvals.
</StepBox>

<StepBox number={4} title="Install via approved vendor and complete net metering">
Ensure the vendor is approved/empanelled and that the system is commissioned after DISCOM inspection. Commissioning proof is key for subsidy release.
</StepBox>

<StepBox number={5} title="Add bank details for DBT after commissioning">
After commissioning approval, enter your bank details on the portal to receive the subsidy via DBT (where applicable).
</StepBox>

<InfoBox>
Practical advice: keep a folder with the latest bill, ID/address proof, and bank IFSC. Many “pending” cases are solved quickly if you can re-upload correct documents immediately.
</InfoBox>
